Reader Picks

disclaimer: yes I am a little odd

  • The Count of Monte Cristo

    Classic book and a wonderful tale of betrayal, loss, love, vengeance, and so much more. The movies are great but do not do the actual story justice. A lesson on how diversity and hardship can make a person change and grow into something greater than they would have been.

    Alexander Dumas

  • The Ethos Effect

    Amazing read and hard to talk about without giving away to much. Wonderful and engaging storytelling. More of a space opera than anything else. The author always does a fantastic job fleshing out his characters and making them very relatable. I also love the way the author touches on societal issues/topics in a very subtle way in all of his books. I cannot give to many details as it is a sequal and would ruin the first book. Amazing author though does sci-fi/fantasy.

    L.E. Modesitt Jr.

  • The picture of Dorian Grey

    I would like to admit right now I have only audio booked this. I have attempted to consume this tale in the traditional sense and I just cannot do it. Great book amazing dialogue classic. This is an amazing way to take a look into the daily life of higher society individuals. For example smoking opium laced tabacoo was a normal occurance.

    Oscar Wilde

  • The Sea-Wolf

    Honestly way better than "The Call of the Wild" in my opinion.Jack London always seems to love taking people who are in a more relaxed posh life style ripping them from that and thrusting them into a harrowing and dangerous adventure. Wonderful book and definately for you if you like reading about ships at sea, life, death, mutiny, suspence, and all the other things this author is so good at.

    Jack London

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and Practicum's library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 visitors each month. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and making use of our ample online resources.

The library gets its name from Joan Feynman, an American astrophysicist and the first woman to be elected as an officer of the American Geophysical Union.
